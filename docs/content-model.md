## 4. ESG Strategy & Sustainability Roadmap

### Journey origin
ESG journey began post-divestiture from Hinduja Global Solutions (Jan 2022). Diagnostic study by a Big 4 firm; phased, structured roadmap since.

### Guiding structure (three pillars, each with Commitment → Key Actions)
- **Environment:** Care for the planet; reduce carbon emissions; optimize resources. Actions: SBTi commitment, track Scopes 1–3, renewables, water recycling, landfill diversion.
- **Social:** Employee & community welfare; responsible employer. Actions: wellbeing, DEI & hybrid culture, human capital investment, equitable grievance handling, CSR in education/sustainability/community.
- **Governance:** Good governance, integrity, ethics in daily practice. Actions: transparency, data security, mandatory privacy training, supplier alignment.

### Materiality assessment
- Conducted FY2023–24, valid through FY2027–28 (3-year cycle). 17 material topics across E/S/G, plotted on a 2×2 (stakeholder influence × business impact).
- **Environment topics:** Climate Change, Energy Management, Water & Waste Management
- **Social topics:** Client Satisfaction & Innovation, DEI, Health/Safety/Wellbeing, Human Capital Management, Employee Engagement, Human Rights, CSR
- **Governance topics:** Cybersecurity & Data Privacy, Compliance, Responsible Procurement, Ethics & Integrity, Risk Management, Stakeholder Grievance Redressal, Corporate Governance
- Highest priority (top-right of matrix): Ethics & Integrity, Compliance, Cybersecurity & Data Privacy, Climate Change, Health Safety & Wellbeing, DEI, Client Satisfaction & Innovation

### Stakeholder engagement (5 groups — table content)
Employees (portals, townhalls, surveys — regular) · Clients (business reviews — regular) · Investors (calls, disclosures — quarterly) · Community (NGO partnerships, volunteering — periodic) · Regulatory bodies (filings — statutory timelines)

### Governance of sustainability (3-tier structure — org diagram)
1. Board-level CSR & Sustainability Committee (≥3 directors, ≥1 independent; Group CFO = Sustainability Head)
2. Sustainability Core Group / Council (executive level, presided by CEO)
3. Sustainability Working Groups (cross-functional: Environment, Social, Governance)

## 15. Content Cautions (consultant notes)

1. **Attrition (27.5%) and POSH cases (13)** are disclosed in the report but are not marketing content. Include only in the ESG Overview scorecard for credibility/transparency, with the report's own context notes — do not feature on hero sections.
2. **Water consumption doubling** (46,850 → 99,463 kL) reflects expanded reporting coverage, not increased use — always pair with the coverage footnote or it reads as regression.
3. **Scope 2 location-based rose 4.23%** — the report frames this against business growth; use market-based figures for headline claims, as the report does.
4. **eNPS dipped 52 → 51** — report explains recalibration; avoid presenting as trend without context.
5. **"150 million transactions"** (FY24 report & FY25 glance page) vs "119+ million claims processed" (FY25 footprint page) — inconsistent in the sources; use "119+ million claims processed annually" (the assured FY25 figure) and "78+ million customer interactions".
6. **FY24 CSAT/eNPS both = 52** — correct per report, not a typo.
7. Client names are never disclosed (only roles) — maintain this in testimonials.
8. FY24 data breach figure was misquoted in the FY24 report and corrected in FY25 — always use FY25's restated values.
9. All content is public-report-sourced; no confidential material. Site should link to the assured PDFs as authoritative sources.
